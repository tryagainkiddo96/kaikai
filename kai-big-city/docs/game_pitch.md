# Game Pitch

## One-Liner

You are Kai, a bold little Shiba Inu loose in a lively city block, turning errands into adventures and every corner into a playground.

## Player Fantasy

The player should feel clever, cute, and slightly chaotic. Kai is not saving the world. Kai is making the neighborhood more memorable by existing in it.

## Why This Works

- Small scope compared with combat-heavy action games
- Strong personality can carry simple mechanics
- Exploration and interaction are easier to polish than large combat systems
- A dense, vertical neighborhood is realistic for a solo project

## Mood

- Warm
- Curious
- Slightly chaotic
- Urban cozy
- Playful, not childish

## Core Loop

1. Spot something interesting.
2. Reach it through movement, climbing, squeezing through spaces, or interacting.
3. Cause a small event or help someone.
4. Earn a collectible, unlock a route, or reveal a new point of interest.
5. Move on to the next curiosity.

## Prototype Slice

The first playable slice should fit inside one dense block with:

- a corner store front
- a small alley
- a rooftop shortcut
- a tiny park or seating area
- 10 to 20 collectibles
- 2 to 3 NPCs
- 1 simple errand chain
