# Poplar Bluff Inspired District Plan

This level should feel inspired by a small Missouri hometown rather than a giant anonymous city. The goal is not a 1:1 recreation. The goal is a compact, game-friendly district that feels believable, local, and personal.

## Direction

Use Poplar Bluff as emotional and structural reference:

- small-town streets
- practical storefronts
- parking lots and side alleys
- rail or industrial edge energy
- older brick buildings
- patches of greenery and civic spaces
- a place that feels lived in instead of glamorous

The game version should be a stylized neighborhood slice built for dog exploration.

## Personal Starting Point

The opening district should be inspired by the area around `1302 N Tenth St, Poplar Bluff, Missouri`, since that is where Kai first lived with you.

Use that location as a private creative anchor for:

- the feeling of the block
- the starting street layout
- the sense of "home turf"
- the opening landmarks and route logic

Do not aim for a literal public-address recreation in the shipped game. The better approach is a softened, stylized neighborhood that captures the memory and vibe of the place.

## Prototype District

The first district should be one tightly connected neighborhood chunk with 5 micro-areas:

1. Main Street Strip
   A short run of storefronts, benches, signs, planters, and sidewalk clutter. This is the social center and the best place for NPC errands.

2. Side Alley
   A narrower service space with fences, dumpsters, stacked crates, delivery doors, and hidden treats. This is where shortcuts and secret routes begin.

3. Pocket Park
   A small green space with trees, a fountain or statue, picnic tables, and places to sniff or dig. This is where Kai can slow down and discover hidden collectibles.

4. Parking Lot and Back Lot
   Open pavement with cars, curbs, shopping carts, puddles, and a few interactable objects. This gives the level breathing room and adds route variation.

5. Rooftop / Awning Route
   A playful traversal lane above the street using awnings, sheds, stacked boxes, AC units, and fire escapes. This is the high-reward route for hidden items and a strong sense of exploration.

## Landmark Ideas

- corner market or convenience store
- mural wall
- small diner or cafe patio
- barber shop or hardware storefront
- civic sign or local welcome marker
- alley delivery door with a quest item nearby
- rooftop pigeon spot or dog vantage point

## Dog-Scale Design Rules

- Every space should have at least one feature interesting to a dog:
  - scent point
  - snack
  - tiny tunnel
  - climbable object
  - bark reaction
  - suspicious bag or dropped food
- Curbs, steps, planters, low walls, and benches should create readable movement chains.
- Secret paths should feel like "dog advantages" instead of human paths.

## Tone

The district should feel:

- sunny or late-afternoon cozy
- grounded and local
- slightly messy in a charming way
- full of small discoveries

## First Quest Candidate

`The Missing Lunch`

- A shop worker dropped their lunch bag during delivery.
- Kai follows scent clues across the block.
- The bag ended up in the alley after crows or a gust knocked it away.
- Completing the errand unlocks a treat reward and a hat or bandana cosmetic.

## Opening Beat

Kai begins near his home block, in a calm familiar space with a sidewalk, yard edge, parked cars, and a nearby route into the wider neighborhood.

The opening should communicate:

- this is Kai's territory
- the world is approachable and worth exploring
- small details matter
- the neighborhood opens up in layers as the player wanders away from home

## Collectible Ideas

- treats
- shiny tags
- lost keys
- toy balls
- wearable bandanas
- "best dog" tokens

## Build Constraint

Keep the first district tiny enough that the player can cross it in under one minute, but dense enough that they can spend 10 to 15 minutes exploring every route.
